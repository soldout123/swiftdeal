Drivers must maintain a positive balance in their SwiftDeal Wallet to receive and accept ride requests on the platform.

How the Wallet Works:

  • Drivers top up their SwiftDeal Wallet by purchasing consumable "SwiftDeal Credits" via Google Play Billing (Android) or Apple In-App Purchases (iOS)
  • The minimum top-up amount is $5.00 USD (or the equivalent in the driver's local currency, dynamically converted based on their detected location)
  • When a driver completes a ride, SwiftDeal automatically deducts a 10% lead-generation commission from the driver's wallet balance
  • The passenger pays the full ride fare directly to the driver in cash — the 10% commission is deducted from the driver's prepaid wallet, not from the cash fare
  • If the driver's wallet balance drops to zero or below, they will be unable to go online or accept new ride requests until they top up

Commission Structure:

  • Commission rate: 10% of the total ride fare on each completed trip
  • The commission is a lead-generation fee for connecting drivers with passengers through the SwiftDeal platform
  • Commission is calculated based on the agreed fare between passenger and driver, and is deducted automatically upon trip completion
  • Drivers can view their full wallet transaction history (top-ups and deductions) within the app at any time

Wallet Credit Terms:

  • SwiftDeal Wallet credits are non-refundable once purchased
  • Wallet credits hold no cash value outside the SwiftDeal platform and cannot be exchanged for cash, transferred to another user, or redeemed in any other form
  • Wallet credits are denominated in the driver's local currency and are used solely to pay platform commission fees
  • SwiftDeal reserves the right to modify commission rates with 30 days' advance notice to active drivers
